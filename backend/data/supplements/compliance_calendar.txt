TAX COMPLIANCE CALENDAR — INDIA AY 2025-26 AND AY 2026-27

This document covers all major tax compliance deadlines for individual taxpayers, businesses, and employers under the Income Tax Act and GST laws, along with penalties for non-compliance.

---

## Income Tax Return (ITR) Filing Deadlines

### Section 139(1) — Original Return (Due Date)

The due date for filing ITR depends on the category of taxpayer:

**Non-Audit Cases:**
- Individuals, HUFs, firms (not requiring audit), BOIs, AOPs
- Due date: July 31 of the Assessment Year
- For AY 2025-26: July 31, 2025
- For AY 2026-27: July 31, 2026

**Audit Cases:**
- Companies, firms, LLPs, and other assessees required to get accounts audited
- Due date: October 31 of the Assessment Year
- For AY 2025-26: October 31, 2025
- For AY 2026-27: October 31, 2026

**Transfer Pricing Cases:**
- Assessees required to furnish report under Section 92E (international transactions)
- Due date: November 30 of the Assessment Year
- For AY 2025-26: November 30, 2025

**Note on Extensions:** The CBDT has historically extended deadlines — often for technical reasons (portal issues) or government notifications. Always check for current-year circulars. However, plan for the statutory deadline.

---

### Section 139(5) — Revised Return

A taxpayer who has filed an original return and discovers any omission or incorrect statement can file a revised return.

**Deadline:** On or before December 31 of the Assessment Year, or before the completion of assessment, whichever is earlier.

**For AY 2025-26:** December 31, 2025
**For AY 2026-27:** December 31, 2026

**Important:** A revised return replaces the original return. There is no limit on the number of revisions within the deadline. There is no penalty for filing a revised return — it is encouraged to correct errors.

---

### Section 139(4) — Belated Return

A taxpayer who misses the original due date can still file a "belated return."

**Deadline:** On or before December 31 of the Assessment Year (same as revised return deadline)

**Consequences of Belated Filing:**
- Late filing fee under Section 234F applies (see penalty section)
- Cannot carry forward most losses (business losses, speculation losses, capital losses) — however, unabsorbed depreciation and losses from house property can still be carried forward
- Interest under Section 234A on tax due

---

### Section 139(8A) — Updated Return (ITR-U)

Introduced by the Finance Act 2022, Section 139(8A) allows taxpayers to file an updated return to report additional income not reported in original/revised returns.

**Filing Window:** Within 24 months from the end of the relevant Assessment Year.

**For AY 2024-25:** Updatable until March 31, 2027
**For AY 2025-26:** Updatable until March 31, 2028

**Additional Tax Payable under Section 140B:**
- If ITR-U filed within 12 months from end of AY: 25% additional tax on tax and interest due
- If ITR-U filed between 12 and 24 months from end of AY: 50% additional tax on tax and interest due

**Conditions:**
- Cannot reduce tax liability (only for declaring additional income)
- Cannot result in refund
- Cannot be filed if: search/survey initiated, assessment proceedings pending, or income related to undisclosed asset/foreign income
- Starting AY 2025-26 (FY 2024-25), Finance Act 2025 has extended the ITR-U window to 48 months with higher additional tax rates for later years (50% for year 3, 60% for year 4)

## Advance Tax Deadlines — Section 207 to 219

Advance tax is required to be paid by taxpayers whose estimated tax liability for the year exceeds ₹10,000 after TDS.

**Installment Schedule and Rates:**

| Installment | Due Date | Cumulative Minimum % |
|------------|----------|---------------------|
| First      | June 15   | 15%                 |
| Second     | September 15 | 45%            |
| Third      | December 15 | 75%             |
| Fourth     | March 15   | 100%               |

**Applicable to:** All taxpayers including individuals, firms, companies.

**Exemption:** Senior citizens (60 years or above) having no income from business or profession are NOT required to pay advance tax.

**Presumptive Taxation (44AD/44ADA/44AE):** Taxpayers under presumptive schemes can pay the entire advance tax by March 15 in a single installment (no June/September/December installments required).

**Interest for Default:**
- Section 234B: Interest at 1% per month on unpaid advance tax (if tax paid is less than 90% of assessed tax by March 31)
- Section 234C: Interest at 1% per month for shortfall in installments at June 15, September 15, December 15, and March 15

## TDS Compliance Calendar

### Deduction and Deposit

**General Rule:** TDS deducted must be deposited with the government by the 7th of the following month.

**Exception for March:** TDS deducted in March must be deposited by April 30 (not April 7).

| Month of Deduction | Deposit Deadline |
|-------------------|-----------------|
| April             | May 7           |
| May               | June 7          |
| June              | July 7          |
| July              | August 7        |
| August            | September 7     |
| September         | October 7       |
| October           | November 7      |
| November          | December 7      |
| December          | January 7       |
| January           | February 7      |
| February          | March 7         |
| March             | April 30        |

**Government Deductors:** Must deposit TDS on the same day as deduction (no 7-day grace period).

### TDS Return Filing Deadlines

Quarterly TDS returns must be filed for each quarter:

| Quarter | Period    | Due Date     |
|---------|-----------|-------------|
| Q1      | Apr–Jun   | July 31     |
| Q2      | Jul–Sep   | October 31  |
| Q3      | Oct–Dec   | January 31  |
| Q4      | Jan–Mar   | May 31      |

**Forms:**
- Form 24Q: TDS from salary payments
- Form 26Q: TDS on all non-salary payments to residents (contractors, professionals, rent, etc.)
- Form 27Q: TDS on payments to non-residents
- Form 27EQ: Tax Collected at Source (TCS) returns

### TDS Certificates

- Form 16 (from employer to employee): Due by June 15 of the AY
- Form 16A (non-salary TDS): Due by 15th of the month following each quarter end (August 15, November 15, February 15, June 15)

## GST Compliance Calendar

### GSTR-1 — Outward Supplies Return

**Monthly filers (turnover > ₹5 crore or opted for monthly filing):**
- Due date: 11th of the following month
- Example: October supplies → filed by November 11

**Quarterly filers (QRMP scheme — turnover up to ₹5 crore):**
- Due date: 13th of the month following the quarter
- Q1 (Apr-Jun): July 13; Q2 (Jul-Sep): October 13; Q3 (Oct-Dec): January 13; Q4 (Jan-Mar): April 13

### GSTR-3B — Summary Return and Tax Payment

**Monthly filers (turnover > ₹5 crore):**
- Due date: 20th of the following month
- Example: October → November 20

**Quarterly filers (QRMP):**
- Due date: 22nd of month following quarter for Category X states; 24th for Category Y states
- States in Category X (generally): Chhattisgarh, MP, Gujarat, Maharashtra, Karnataka, Goa, Kerala, TN, Telangana, AP, and others
- States in Category Y (generally): Himachal Pradesh, Punjab, Uttarakhand, Haryana, Rajasthan, UP, Bihar, Sikkim, AR, Nagaland, Manipur, Mizoram, Tripura, Meghalaya, Assam, West Bengal, Jharkhand, Odisha, J&K, Ladakh

**QRMP Intermediate Payments:**
- QRMP filers must pay tax for months 1 and 2 of the quarter by the 25th of the next month (called Invoice Furnishing Facility/IFF and PMT-06 payments)

### GSTR-9 — Annual Return

- Applicable to regular taxpayers
- Due date: December 31 of the following financial year
- For FY 2024-25: December 31, 2025
- Taxpayers with turnover up to ₹2 crore: Optional (but filing is advisable)
- Taxpayers with turnover above ₹2 crore: Mandatory
- GSTR-9C (Reconciliation Statement) required for taxpayers with turnover above ₹5 crore

### GSTR-8 — TCS Return for E-commerce Operators

- Monthly, due by the 10th of the following month

### GST Payment

- GST must be paid by the 20th of the following month for monthly filers (coincides with GSTR-3B)
- Interest at 18% per annum on delayed payment from the due date

## Tax Audit — Section 44AB

**Who Requires Tax Audit:**
- Business with turnover exceeding ₹1 crore (or ₹10 crore if cash receipts and payments are each ≤5% of total)
- Professional with gross receipts exceeding ₹50 lakh
- Assessees opting out of presumptive taxation and declaring income below prescribed rates

**Report:** Form 3CD with Form 3CA (for those maintaining accounts as required) or Form 3CB

**Due Date:** October 31 of the Assessment Year (same as audit return due date)

---

## Key Penalty Provisions

### Section 234F — Late Filing Fee

A fee (not penalty, but effectively same) applies for late filing of ITR:
- Total income > ₹5 lakh: Fee of ₹5,000
- Total income ≤ ₹5 lakh: Fee of ₹1,000
- Applies only to belated returns filed after the original due date

**Note:** If total income is below the basic exemption limit, no late filing fee is charged even if return is filed late.

### Section 234A — Interest on Default in Filing Return

- Rate: 1% per month (or part of a month) on amount of tax due
- Period: From the day after the due date to the date of actual filing
- Calculated on the tax due after deducting TDS, advance tax paid

### Section 234B — Interest on Default in Advance Tax Payment

- Rate: 1% per month on shortfall in advance tax
- Triggered when total advance tax paid by March 31 is less than 90% of assessed tax
- Period: April 1 to date of actual payment/assessment

### Section 234C — Interest on Deferment of Advance Tax Installments

- Rate: 1% per month on shortfall at each installment date
- Period: 3 months for June and September installments; 1 month for December installment; April 1 to actual payment for March installment

### Section 271(1)(c) — Penalty for Concealment of Income

- Applies when taxpayer conceals income or furnishes inaccurate particulars
- Penalty range: 100% to 300% of tax on concealed income
- This is the most serious penalty provision under direct tax law

### Section 270A — Penalty for Misreporting/Under-reporting of Income

Introduced from AY 2017-18, Section 270A now covers most income understatement cases:

**Under-reporting of income:**
- Penalty: 50% of tax on under-reported income

**Misreporting of income** (more serious — includes false entry, suppression, misrepresentation):
- Penalty: 200% of tax on misreported income

**Key distinction:** Misreporting (200% penalty) is for deliberate falsification, while under-reporting (50% penalty) includes computational errors. If the taxpayer can demonstrate genuine belief or uncertainty, the lower rate may apply.

### Section 272A — Penalty for Failure to File TDS Returns

- Penalty: ₹100 per day for each day of default
- Also applies to failure to furnish TDS certificates (Form 16/16A)

### Section 234E — Fee for Late Filing of TDS/TCS Returns

- ₹200 per day per statement until the return is filed
- But total fee cannot exceed the TDS/TCS amount for the period

### Section 271C — Penalty for Failure to Deduct TDS

- Penalty equal to the amount of TDS not deducted
- In addition, the payer is treated as "assessee in default" and liable for the TDS amount plus interest under Section 201(1A)

### GST Penalties

- Late filing of GSTR: ₹50 per day (₹25 CGST + ₹25 SGST), subject to maximum ₹5,000
- Nil return late filing: ₹20 per day (₹10 CGST + ₹10 SGST), maximum ₹500
- Penalty for evasion: 100% of tax due, minimum ₹10,000
- Interest on delayed GST payment: 18% per annum from due date

## Month-by-Month Compliance Summary

**April:**
- Advance tax first installment — not applicable (no deadline in April, first is June 15)
- Deposit March TDS by April 30

**May:**
- Deposit April TDS by May 7
- File Q4 TDS returns (24Q/26Q/27Q/27EQ) by May 31
- Advance: Set estimated income, compute advance tax for the year

**June:**
- First advance tax installment: June 15 (pay at least 15% of annual liability)
- GST annual return GSTR-9 for previous year: Ideally begin preparation

**July:**
- Deposit June TDS by July 7
- File Q1 TDS returns by July 31
- File ITR for non-audit cases by July 31
- File GSTR-1 for June (monthly filers) by July 11

**August:**
- Issue Form 16A (Q1 TDS certificates) by August 15

**September:**
- Second advance tax installment: September 15 (cumulative 45%)
- GST: GSTR-1 for August by September 11

**October:**
- Deposit September TDS by October 7
- File Q2 TDS returns by October 31
- File ITR for audit cases by October 31
- Complete tax audit (Form 3CD) by October 31

**November:**
- Issue Form 16A for Q2 by November 15
- File ITR for TP cases by November 30

**December:**
- Third advance tax installment: December 15 (cumulative 75%)
- File revised/belated ITR by December 31
- File GST annual return GSTR-9 for previous FY by December 31

**January:**
- Deposit December TDS by January 7
- File Q3 TDS returns by January 31

**February:**
- Issue Form 16A for Q3 by February 15

**March:**
- Final advance tax installment: March 15 (pay 100%)
- Year-end planning: Ensure all investments, payments, and deductible expenses are completed before March 31
- Employees: Submit investment declarations to employer for accurate TDS computation
