## INCOME BRACKET 1: Below в‚№7 Lakh (Gross Income)

### Tax Situation
Under the new tax regime (AY 2026-27):
- Gross income: в‚№7,00,000
- Standard deduction (salaried): в‚№75,000
- Net taxable income: в‚№6,25,000
- Tax computation: 5% on (в‚№6,25,000 в€’ в‚№4,00,000) = 5% Г— в‚№2,25,000 = в‚№11,250
- Section 87A rebate: Full rebate since income в‰¤ в‚№12 lakh

**Verdict:** At this income level, no tax planning is needed under the new regime. The standard deduction and 87A rebate together eliminate all tax.

### Strategy

- No investments required for tax saving (unlike old regime where you had to invest в‚№1.5L in 80C to reduce tax).
- Focus energy on wealth creation вЂ” invest in equity mutual funds for long-term growth rather than forced tax-saving products.

### When Old Regime Could Apply
- If total deductions (HRA + 80C + 80D + 24(b)) exceed в‚№4.25 lakh (the break-even between regimes at this income), old regime would be better. For income below в‚№7 lakh, this break-even is rarely achievable.

## INCOME BRACKET 5: в‚№50 Lakh to в‚№1 Crore

**Surcharge kicks in at в‚№50 lakh:** 10% surcharge is added to the income tax for income between в‚№50 lakh and в‚№1 crore.

This effectively increases marginal rates by 10%:
- New regime 30% bracket в†’ 30% Г— 1.10 Г— 1.04 = 34.32% effective
- Old regime 30% bracket в†’ 30% Г— 1.10 Г— 1.04 = 34.32% effective

### New Regime Tax at в‚№75 Lakh
Taxable income (after в‚№75K std deduction): в‚№74,25,000
Tax before surcharge: compute on slabs в†’ approximately в‚№18.5L
Surcharge @ 10%: в‚№1.85L
Cess @ 4% on (в‚№18.5L + в‚№1.85L): в‚№81,400
Total tax: ~в‚№21.16L (effective rate: ~28.2%)

### Key Strategies at в‚№50LвЂ“в‚№1Cr:
1. **Maximize 80CCD(2):** With a в‚№60 lakh salary, employer NPS at 10% = в‚№6 lakh deduction in old regime (and new regime). At 34.32% effective rate, this saves в‚№2.06 lakh in taxes annually.

2. **HUF Formation:** Creating a Hindu Undivided Family (HUF) creates a separate tax entity with its own basic exemption and slab rates. Ancestral income can be routed through the HUF. The HUF can invest in PPF (separate в‚№1.5L 80C limit), receive rental income from jointly owned ancestral property, etc.

3. **Section 54/54EC on Property Sale:** If selling inherited or long-held property, plan reinvestment in another property (Section 54) or NHAI/REC bonds (Section 54EC вЂ” в‚№50L limit, 5-year lock-in) to reduce or eliminate LTCG.

4. **Income Splitting:** Route investments through spouse (from their independent income). Each person has their own basic exemption. However, ensure no clubbing provisions are triggered.

5. **Section 80G Donations:** Donations to PM Relief Fund, National Foundation for Communal Harmony, etc. give 100% deduction. Donations to other registered trusts give 50%. Eligible deduction reduces the taxable income in the 34.32% bracket, making philanthropy tax-efficient.

## INCOME BRACKET 6: в‚№1 Crore to в‚№2 Crore

**15% surcharge** applies to income between в‚№1 crore and в‚№2 crore.

Effective marginal rate: 30% Г— 1.15 Г— 1.04 = 35.88%

**This is a meaningful jump from the 34.32% at в‚№50LвЂ“в‚№1Cr range.**

### High-Impact Strategies at в‚№1вЂ“в‚№2 Crore:

1. **Company structure for business income:** If a significant portion of income is from business (not salary), routing through a private limited company taxed at 22% (under Section 115BAA) can save over 10 percentage points of effective tax. The promoter draws a reasonable salary from the company and keeps retained earnings in the company for reinvestment.

2. **ESOP management:** Exercise ESOPs in tranches to stay in lower surcharge brackets in each year rather than exercising all in one year.

3. **Section 80JJAA (New Employment Deduction):** If the taxpayer has a business and has recruited new employees, 30% of additional employee cost is deductible for 3 years. A business owner hiring 10 employees at в‚№5L each can claim в‚№1.5L Г— 30% Г— 3 years of additional deduction.

4. **Capital gains harvesting:** LTCG on equity is taxable at 12.5% (Section 112A) regardless of income level, with no surcharge for listed equity. Strategically realizing long-term capital gains (selling and rebuying) can be an efficient way to "consume" the в‚№1.25L annual exemption and reset cost base at current prices.

5. **Section 54F:** If making any sale of a non-house asset generating LTCG, reinvest in residential property to claim Section 54F exemption.

## INCOME BRACKET 7: Above в‚№2 Crore

**25% surcharge** applies to income above в‚№2 crore (up to в‚№5 crore).

Effective marginal rate: 30% Г— 1.25 Г— 1.04 = 39%

**Note:** The Finance Act 2023 reduced the surcharge cap for the new regime from 37% to 25% (for income above в‚№5 crore). Under the old regime, income above в‚№5 crore still carries 37% surcharge (effective 42.7%). This makes the new regime structurally more attractive at very high incomes.

### New Regime Advantage Above в‚№2 Crore:
For income above в‚№5 crore:
- New regime: 30% tax + 25% surcharge = effective 39% (max)
- Old regime: 30% tax + 37% surcharge = effective 42.7% (max)
- Difference: 3.7 percentage points on income above в‚№5 crore

At в‚№10 crore income, the new regime can save approximately в‚№18.5 lakh in tax compared to the old regime even without accounting for any deductions.

### Key Strategies Above в‚№2 Crore:

1. **Salary restructuring is critical:** Every rupee converted from taxable salary to exempt allowance or deductible component saves 39%+ in tax. Priority items:
   - NPS employer contribution (10% of salary) вЂ” deductible in new regime
   - Rent-free accommodation vs HRA (evaluate which is more tax-efficient)
   - Car perquisite vs reimbursement
   - ESOPs вЂ” defer exercise if possible

2. **Company or LLP for business income:** At 39% personal tax vs 25.17% corporate tax, retaining profits in a company saves ~14 percentage points. Over time, the retained profits can be invested, compounded, and used for business growth before eventual distribution.

3. **Family trust/HUF:** Complex estate planning and income distribution strategies involving HUF and family trusts (Section 10(26AAA) etc.) can be explored with a qualified tax advisor.

4. **Section 80JJAA:** For business owners, new employee hiring deduction continues to be a significant benefit.

5. **Charitable giving:** Donations to institutions eligible for 100% deduction (Section 80G) reduce income at the highest marginal rate вЂ” every в‚№1 lakh donated saves в‚№39,000 in tax at 39% effective rate.

6. **Agricultural income planning:** Where genuine agricultural operations are possible, agricultural income is exempt. Integration of agricultural operations with business structures can be explored.

## Universal Action Items Regardless of Income Level

1. **File ITR on time:** Even if no tax is due, filing by July 31 preserves your right to carry forward losses and avoids the в‚№1,000вЂ“в‚№5,000 late filing fee.

2. **Check Form 26AS and AIS:** Reconcile TDS credits before filing. Discrepancies can be resolved proactively.

3. **Switch regimes annually:** Each year you can evaluate and choose the better regime based on that year's income and deductions. There is no permanent commitment.

4. **NPS contribution:** Employer NPS (80CCD(2)) is available in new regime. Employee own NPS (80CCD(1B)) is only beneficial in old regime. Prioritize employer NPS restructuring.

5. **Health insurance:** Section 80D deductions for parents are often overlooked. Even if deduction isn't the primary reason, adequate health insurance is essential and provides tax benefit in old regime.
