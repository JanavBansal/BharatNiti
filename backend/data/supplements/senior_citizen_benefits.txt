SENIOR CITIZEN TAX BENEFITS IN INDIA — AY 2025-26 AND AY 2026-27
Income Tax Act, 1961 — Comprehensive Guide for Senior and Super Senior Citizens

## Who Is a Senior Citizen for Income Tax Purposes?

The Income Tax Act classifies senior citizens into two categories based on age:

1. Senior Citizen: An individual who is 60 years or above but below 80 years of age at any time during the relevant financial year
2. Super Senior Citizen: An individual who is 80 years or above at any time during the relevant financial year

The age must be completed DURING the financial year (not at the start). If a person turns 60 on March 30, 2025, they are a senior citizen for AY 2025-26 (FY 2024-25) for the entire year.

Non-Resident Indians (NRIs) who are senior citizens do NOT get the higher basic exemption limit under the Old Tax Regime — the enhanced exemption is only for Resident senior citizens.

## Higher Basic Exemption Limits — Old Tax Regime

The basic exemption limits under the Old Tax Regime (Section 2(14) and the Finance Act):

For Individuals below 60 years: Rs. 2,50,000
For Senior Citizens (60-80 years, Residents): Rs. 3,00,000
For Super Senior Citizens (80+, Residents): Rs. 5,00,000

This means:
- A senior citizen pays no tax on income up to Rs. 3L (old regime)
- A super senior citizen pays no tax on income up to Rs. 5L (old regime)

Under the New Tax Regime (Section 115BAC), the basic exemption limit is Rs. 3,00,000 for ALL individuals regardless of age. The higher limits of Rs. 3L and Rs. 5L are ONLY available under the Old Tax Regime.

### Rebate Under Section 87A:

Under the Old Tax Regime: Rebate of up to Rs. 12,500 if total income is Rs. 5,00,000 or below. This effectively means zero tax for senior citizens with income up to Rs. 5L.

Under the New Tax Regime: Rebate of up to Rs. 25,000 if total income is Rs. 7,00,000 or below. Since the standard deduction for salaried seniors is Rs. 75,000 under NTR, a pensioner with pension income up to Rs. 7,75,000 effectively pays zero tax under NTR.

## Tax Slabs for Senior Citizens (Old Tax Regime, AY 2025-26)

For Senior Citizens (60-79 years):
- Up to Rs. 3,00,000: NIL
- Rs. 3,00,001 to Rs. 5,00,000: 5%
- Rs. 5,00,001 to Rs. 10,00,000: 20%
- Above Rs. 10,00,000: 30%

For Super Senior Citizens (80 years and above):
- Up to Rs. 5,00,000: NIL
- Rs. 5,00,001 to Rs. 10,00,000: 20%
- Above Rs. 10,00,000: 30%

Surcharge: 10% for income Rs. 50L-1 crore; 15% for Rs. 1-2 crore; 25% for Rs. 2-5 crore; 37% above Rs. 5 crore
Health and Education Cess: 4% on (tax + surcharge)

For super senior citizens, the nil slab up to Rs. 5L combined with Section 87A rebate means even lower tax. Note: Super senior citizens do NOT get the Section 87A rebate separately because their exemption limit itself covers the income range — the rebate is relevant for senior citizens (60-79) with income up to Rs. 5L.

## Section 80TTB — Interest Deduction for Senior Citizens

Section 80TTB (introduced from FY 2018-19) provides a deduction of up to Rs. 50,000 for interest income earned by senior citizens from:
- Savings bank accounts
- Fixed deposits with banks and post offices
- Recurring deposits

This replaces Section 80TTA (which gives only Rs. 10,000 deduction on savings account interest) for senior citizens. Non-senior citizens use Section 80TTA (Rs. 10,000 on savings account interest only); senior citizens use Section 80TTB (Rs. 50,000 on all bank/post office interest).

Example: A senior citizen has FD interest of Rs. 60,000 and savings account interest of Rs. 5,000. Total interest = Rs. 65,000. Section 80TTB deduction = Rs. 50,000. Net taxable = Rs. 15,000.

Note: Senior citizens CANNOT simultaneously claim Section 80TTA. Section 80TTB subsumes 80TTA for them.

Section 80TTB is available under the Old Tax Regime ONLY. Under the New Tax Regime, neither 80TTA nor 80TTB is available.

## Section 80D — Enhanced Medical Insurance Deduction

Senior citizens get a higher deduction limit for health insurance premiums under Section 80D:

For Non-Senior Citizens:
- Self + family: Up to Rs. 25,000 per year
- Parents (non-senior): Additional Rs. 25,000

For Senior Citizens:
- If the senior citizen is the taxpayer (or covered under the policy): Limit is Rs. 50,000
- If parents are senior citizens: Additional Rs. 50,000 for parents' policy (total Rs. 1,00,000 if both the taxpayer and parents are senior)

### Uninsured Senior Citizen Parent Medical Expenditure:
If a senior citizen parent is uninsured (no health insurance policy), medical expenditure actually incurred for treatment of the senior citizen parent is deductible up to Rs. 50,000 under the proviso to Section 80D. This is in lieu of the insurance premium deduction.

### Maximum Possible Section 80D Deduction for Senior Citizen Taxpayer:
- Senior citizen taxpayer: Rs. 50,000 (own/family policy)
- Senior citizen parents: Rs. 50,000 (parents' policy or medical bills if uninsured)
- Total maximum: Rs. 1,00,000

Section 80D is available under the Old Tax Regime only.

## Section 80DDB — Specified Disease Deduction (Enhanced for Senior Citizens)

Section 80DDB provides a deduction for expenditure on treatment of specified diseases for self or dependents.

Specified diseases include:
- Neurological diseases (Dementia, Dystonia Musculorum Deformans, Motor Neuron Disease, Ataxia, Chorea, Hemiballismus, Aphasia, Parkinson's disease) — where disability is 40%+
- Cancer (Malignant Cancers)
- Chronic Renal Failure (Full Renal Failure)
- AIDS (Acquired Immuno-Deficiency Syndrome)
- Bone Marrow Transplant (Haematological disorders)

Deduction Limits:
- For non-senior citizen taxpayer treating a non-senior dependent: Rs. 40,000
- For senior citizen taxpayer or treating a senior citizen dependent: Rs. 1,00,000

The deduction is the ACTUAL expenditure paid, subject to the above limits, reduced by any amount reimbursed by insurance or employer.

A certificate in Form 10-I from a specialist in a government hospital (or recognized private hospital) is required to claim this deduction.

Section 80DDB is available under the Old Tax Regime only.

## No Advance Tax Obligation — Section 207

Section 207 provides a significant compliance relief for senior citizens:

A senior citizen (60 years or above) who is a RESIDENT INDIAN and does NOT have any income from business or profession is EXEMPT from paying advance tax.

This means:
- If income is only from pension, interest, rental income, capital gains, dividends
- The entire tax can be paid as self-assessment tax before filing the return
- No interest under Section 234B or 234C for failure to pay advance tax

Exceptions — Advance Tax IS still required if:
- The senior citizen has any income from business or profession
- The senior citizen is a non-resident (NRI seniors must pay advance tax)

This exemption gives senior citizens with investment income tremendous flexibility — they can calculate tax liability at year end and pay in one shot, avoiding the quarterly installment discipline.

Note: Interest under Section 234A still applies if the return is filed late.

## Reverse Mortgage — Not Taxable

Reverse Mortgage is a product offered by banks where a senior citizen mortgages their residential property to the bank in exchange for a stream of periodic payments (monthly, quarterly, or lump sum). The senior continues to live in the house.

Under Section 10(43) of the Income Tax Act:
- The amount received under a reverse mortgage scheme notified by the Central Government is NOT treated as income of the individual
- The periodic payments are treated as loan proceeds, not income — hence fully tax-free

The scheme must be under the Pradhan Mantri Gram Sadak Yojana or any scheme notified by the Central Government. Reverse mortgage from scheduled commercial banks and housing finance companies under the NHB guidelines qualifies.

At the time of the borrower's death (or when they permanently move out), the bank sells the property and recovers the loan. Any surplus after loan recovery goes to the heirs. The heirs may have capital gains implications on this final sale, but the periodic payments during the reverse mortgage period are tax-free.

## Form 15H — Avoiding TDS on Interest

Senior citizens can submit Form 15H to their bank to prevent TDS from being deducted on interest income, provided their estimated total tax liability for the year is NIL.

### Conditions for Valid Form 15H:
1. The submitter must be a RESIDENT senior citizen (60 years or above)
2. The estimated total income for the year must be at or below the taxable limit (i.e., the tax computed on total income should be NIL after all deductions and exemptions)
3. Form 15H can be submitted even if the gross interest exceeds the exemption limit, as long as AFTER deductions (80C, 80TTB, etc.) the net taxable income results in NIL tax

For super senior citizens with income up to Rs. 5L: Form 15H is readily available since basic exemption itself covers the income.

Non-senior citizens use Form 15G (where the additional condition is that gross interest cannot exceed the basic exemption limit — stricter condition).

### How to Submit Form 15H:
- Submit at the beginning of each financial year to each bank branch where FDs/savings are maintained
- Can now be submitted online through the bank's net banking portal
- The bank sends a consolidated report to the Income Tax Department — Form 15H submissions are tracked

### TDS Threshold for Banks Under Section 194A:
Even without Form 15H, TDS is deducted only when interest income from a SINGLE BANK (all branches combined) exceeds:
- Rs. 50,000 per year for senior citizens (Rs. 40,000 for non-seniors)

If interest is below Rs. 50,000 from a bank, no TDS is deducted — Form 15H is only needed when interest exceeds Rs. 50,000.

## Senior Citizens Savings Scheme (SCSS) — Section 80C Eligible

SCSS is a government-backed savings scheme specifically for senior citizens (60+) and eligible retirees (55-60, for those who took VRS or superannuation).

Key features:
- Maximum deposit: Rs. 30,00,000 (enhanced from Rs. 15L in Budget 2023)
- Interest rate: Set quarterly by government, currently in the 8-8.2% range
- Tenure: 5 years (extendable by 3 years)
- Eligible investors: Individuals 60+ years; 55-60 years if retired under VRS/superannuation within 1 month
- Section 80C deduction: Amount invested in SCSS is eligible for Rs. 1.5L annual limit under 80C
- Interest: Fully taxable at slab rate; TDS applies if interest > Rs. 50,000 per year (Form 15H can be submitted)

SCSS is one of the highest-returning government-guaranteed schemes. The maximum investment of Rs. 30L at ~8% gives Rs. 2.4L annual interest, providing regular income.

## Pradhan Mantri Vaya Vandana Yojana (PMVVY)

PMVVY is an annuity scheme offered through LIC for senior citizens (60+), providing assured pension and guaranteed returns.

Key features:
- Mode: Monthly, quarterly, half-yearly, or annual pension
- Policy Term: 10 years
- Return: Guaranteed return (8% per annum equivalent at launch; scheme has been closed for new subscriptions after March 31, 2023)
- Status for new investors: Closed as of March 31, 2023 — existing policyholders continue

Tax treatment:
- Premium paid: NOT eligible for Section 80C deduction (unlike some other pension plans)
- Pension received: Fully taxable as income from other sources at applicable slab rate
- On death of policyholder: Purchase price returned to nominee, not taxable

## Additional Senior Citizen Benefits

### Standard Deduction for Pensioners:
Pension from a former employer is treated as salary income. Senior citizens receiving pension can claim the standard deduction of Rs. 50,000 (Old Regime) or Rs. 75,000 (New Regime from AY 2025-26), just like salaried individuals.

### Family Pension:
Family pension received by widow/widower/dependents of a deceased employee is taxable as "Income from Other Sources." Deduction of lower of Rs. 15,000 or 1/3rd of family pension is available under Section 57.

### Special Provision for Very Senior Citizens (80+) Filing Returns:
Super senior citizens have the option to file ITR on paper (physical submission) even when mandatory e-filing applies to others. However, e-filing is encouraged and simpler. From AY 2023-24, ITR-1 and ITR-4 can be filed manually for super senior citizens.

### Capital Gains for Senior Citizens:
Senior citizens do NOT get any special exemption from capital gains tax. Capital gains (STCG/LTCG) are taxed at the same rates as for any other taxpayer. The higher basic exemption limit (Rs. 3L / Rs. 5L) can be adjusted against non-special-rate income. For income taxed at special rates (LTCG at 12.5%, STCG at 20%), the basic exemption limit is adjusted against normal income first.

### Section 139(1) proviso — Exemption from Filing:
Senior citizens and super senior citizens earning only pension and interest from specified banks are exempt from filing ITR if the bank deducts TDS after applying all eligible deductions. This was introduced by Budget 2021. The senior citizen must file Form 12BBA with the bank. The bank then deducts correct TDS and files a return on behalf of the senior citizen.

This facility is available for senior citizens (75+) who:
- Have only pension income from a specified bank
- Have only interest income from the same bank
- File Form 12BBA with the bank certifying these facts

## Key Sections Reference

- Section 2(14) and Finance Act: Age-based classification
- Section 80C: General savings deduction (Rs. 1.5L limit)
- Section 80D: Health insurance premium deduction
- Section 80DDB: Deduction for specified diseases
- Section 80TTB: Interest deduction for senior citizens (Rs. 50,000)
- Section 87A: Rebate for low-income taxpayers
- Section 115BAC: New tax regime (no age-based exemption difference)
- Section 194A: TDS on interest (Rs. 50,000 threshold for seniors)
- Section 207: No advance tax for resident senior citizens (no business income)
- Section 10(43): Reverse mortgage receipt not taxable
- Section 139(1) proviso: 75+ seniors filing exemption via Form 12BBA
